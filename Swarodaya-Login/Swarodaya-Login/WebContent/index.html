Swaryog

"The one who understands one's breathe and its subtle elements, has known the Vedas" - Shrimad Bhagwat.

Swar Yog, the ancient Indian science of breath awareness has been practiced since ages by yogis of India to make their lives more meaningful and fulfilling. Swar Yog helps you in a way that rarely any other science can accomplish. It helps you to know yourself on various levels and with that with that knowledge helps you to create abundance at all levels. Swarodaya Shastra, an authorative text on this subject is written as a conversation between Lord Shiva and his consort, Parvati.

ShivSwarodaya Shastra, there are three main Nadis in the human body : The Chandra Or the Ida Nadi, The Surya Or the Pingala Nadi, and The Shunya Or the Sushumna Nadi. The Nadis operate alternately for certain duration of time in every hour. The balance flow of breath in these three Nadis provides us with health, peace and prosperity in life.

Chandra Swar or Ida Nadi

The left nostril is called Chandra Swar or the Ida Nadi is the feminine Nadi. It is ruled by the Moon and is associated with the functioning of the right brain. When the flow of Ida is dominant, we are better prepared for mental tasks.

Surya Swar or Pingala Nadi

The right nostril is called Surya Swar or the Pingala Nadi. It is the masculine Nadi. It is ruled by the Sun and is associated with the functioning of the left brain. When the flow of Pingala is dominant, we are better prepared for physical tasks.

Shunya OR Sushumna Nadi

The third Nadi is called Sushumna Nadi. It is located at the base of the spine. When both nostrils flow together, the Sushumna is said to be active. The flow of Sushumna Nadi indicates the blissful state of the mind.


The science of SwarYog guides us on how we can align one's activities to one's breath rhythm to get maximum benefits in actions. It also guides us to subtle elements of breath and its benefits.
